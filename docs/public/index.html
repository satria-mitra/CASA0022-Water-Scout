What is Water Scout?

The Water Scout project aims to create an affordable, self-powered device that uses ultrasonic sensors and LoRaWAN technology to monitor real-time water levels in urban areas. This device helps improve flood risk assessment by collecting data from the community, filling in gaps left by traditional methods. The data is then displayed on a web dashboard with 3D visualization, making it easier for people to understand water height.

Low-Cost for Mass Deployment

Using only open-source stack and low-cost devices, enabling mass deployment for enriching spatial data.

Real-Time Monitoring

Leveraging WebXR technology, Water Scout's visualizations are immersive and accessible on various platforms, providing an engaging way to view water levels and assess flood risks.

Community Driven

Driven by citizen participation to fortify resilience to flood risk.

Mobile-Friendly

Developed as a web-based application, allowing access from PC or smartphone without a special app.

Get Started

Real-Time Monitoring powered by Arduino with LoRAWAN

Water Scout uses LoRaWAN technology to provide reliable, long-range communication with minimal power consumption, allowing the device to operate for extended periods without maintenance.

It transmits real-time water level data to the web dashboard, where it is visualized in 3D, providing clear and accessible flood information for all users.

WebXR for 3D visualisations

Leveraging WebXR technology, Water Scout's visualizations are immersive and accessible on various platforms, providing an engaging way to view water levels and assess flood risks.

Augmented Reality (AR) Experience

Experience real-time flood data in your environment using AR, making it easier to visualize and understand potential impacts directly from your smartphone or AR-enabled device.
